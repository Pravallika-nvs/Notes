5. Types of Flow

Flow can be classified into two main types: Laminar Flow and Turbulent Flow.

5.1 Laminar Flow

In laminar flow, fluid moves in parallel layers with minimal disruption between them.

Characteristics:

  • Low velocity
  • High viscosity
  • Reynolds number (Re) less than 2100

5.2 Turbulent Flow

Turbulent flow is chaotic and irregular, with eddies and vortices.

Characteristics:

  • High velocity
  • Low viscosity
  • Reynolds number (Re) greater than 4000

Industries mostly deal with turbulent flow, especially in pipelines with:

  • Bends
  • Joints
  • Curves

These cause energy losses, so industries prefer straight-line paths whenever possible.

5.3 Transitional Flow

Transitional flow occurs between laminar and turbulent flow, with Reynolds numbers between 2100 and 4000.

It exhibits characteristics of both laminar and turbulent flow.
