6. Flow Measuring Devices

Flow measuring devices are essential for monitoring and controlling fluid flow in various applications.

Common types include:

  • Orifice Meters
  • Venturi Meters
  • Rotameters
  • Ultrasonic Flow Meters

These devices help in measuring flow rate, pressure, and other parameters critical for system performance.

6.1 Orifice Meters

Orifice meters measure flow by creating a pressure drop across an orifice plate.

Features:

  • Has a thin plate with a hole (orifice).
  • The fluid flows through the cross-sectional area.
  • Plate angle: 30° to 45°
  • Less efficient than Venturimeter but simpler.

6.2 Venturi Meters

Venturi meters measure flow by utilizing the Venturi effect, where fluid velocity increases as it passes through a constricted section of the pipe.

Features:

  • Measures flow rate using converging and diverging sections.
  • Inlet (converging): 16–22°.
  • Outlet (diverging): 9°.
  • Has a coefficient close to 0.98.
  • Commonly used in the petroleum industry.
