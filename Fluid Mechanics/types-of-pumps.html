7. Types of Pumps

Pumps are mechanical devices used to move fluids from one place to another.

They can be classified into two main types: Positive Displacement Pumps and Centrifugal Pumps.

7.1 Positive Displacement Pumps

Positive displacement pumps move fluid by trapping a fixed amount and forcing it into the discharge pipe.

They are of two types: Reciprocating and Rotary Pumps

7.1.1 Reciprocating Pumps

Reciprocating pumps use a piston to move fluid in and out of a cylinder. They work on to-and-fro motion.

Examples include Piston pumps and plunger pumps.

7.1.2 Rotary Pumps

Rotary pumps use rotating mechanisms to move fluid. They work on a continuous motion.

Examples include Gear pumps and Mono pumps.

Gear pumps are used for high-viscosity fluids like grease, or heavy oil fractions.

7.2 Centrifugal Pumps

  • Use centrifugal force to convert kinetic energy into pressure energy.
  • Based on: Kinetic Energy, KE = ½ mv2
  • Suitable for general liquid pumping.
  • Includes Foot Valve at inlet (like a mesh) to block debris and Impeller (blade-like component) – rotates to push fluid smoothly.
