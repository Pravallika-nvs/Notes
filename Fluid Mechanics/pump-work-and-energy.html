4. Pump Work and Energy

Pumps are devices that move fluids by mechanical action.

They convert mechanical energy into hydraulic energy.

However, pumps have energy losses, including:

  • Frictional losses
  • Efficiency losses
  • Losses due to bends and joints in pipelines

The work done by a pump can be calculated using the formula: W = P × Q, where W is work, P is pressure, and Q is flow rate.
